◎The Drill String as a System · How the Products Assemble

These products are not independent — they're concentric layers of one telescoping string. Casing lines the hole; drill rod runs inside the casing and carries the core barrel; the core barrel (next branch) sits inside the rod and captures the sample. This view shows how a matched size stacks together, the clearances between layers, and the product tree as it grows.

Each branch is benchmarked across the same three suppliers (Sino-Tech / Hole Product / Sunnycorner). Sino-Tech is the Boart Longyear line, so it doubles as the OEM/standard reference. Click a category tab above to open a branch; Core Barrel slots in here the same way Casing did.

⇄System Fit · does the rod nest inside its casing?

For each size, the matched casing and drill rod share the same letter (NW casing ↔ N rod). The drill rod's OD must clear the casing's ID — the difference is the diametral clearance. Values use the Sino-Tech (Boart Longyear) standard dimensions.

Clearance is per-diameter (casing ID − rod OD). It must stay positive for the rod to pass, but small — excess clearance wastes hole and lets the rod whip. This is why each size pairs one casing with one rod family.

2Thread Properties & Their Impact

The thread sets joint make/break ease, load efficiency, anti-jamming and life. Boart Longyear's four thread generations are the core IP — from the 1966 Q to the 2018 double-start XQ, load efficiency climbs 30% → 60%. Never mix threads from different makers — catastrophic joint failure and voided warranty.

Why Thread Geometry Matters — animated joint under load

A drill-rod joint is a tapered pin (male) screwed into a box (female). Pick a thread: the left view spins the joint together (make & break), the right view zooms one load flank and pulls the hanging string's weight through it. Watch what the flank does — that is the whole reason thread choice changes depth & life.
TPI = threads per inch — how coarse the thread is. Fewer TPI = bigger, coarser teeth = fewer turns to make up.

① Make & break — how easily it connects
Coarser thread (fewer TPI) = fewer turns to spin together & apart. The pin loops in and out.
② Load flank under string tension — why it holds (or doesn't)
A reverse-angle flank (barb) hooks & locks when pulled; a symmetric flank lets the pin cam loose & slip out.

3W-Wall vs V-Wall · Variable-Wall Showdown

Both are internal-upset variable-wall tubing — thick joints, thin mid-body. Judging by OD alone misleads. The real difference is the wall-transition design: V-Wall steps down abruptly (stress & wear concentrate there), W-Wall transitions gradually (stress spreads → better wear-life & stiffness). The differing zones are highlighted below.

V-Wall — earlier variable-wall design
Supplier: Sino-Tech
Threads: Q / RQ / HD
Effect: −30% weight · larger ID · more depth (NQA 2300 → 3080 m)
Risk: abrupt transition concentrates bending & wear
Cost: lower than premium W-Wall
W-Wall™ — improved variable-wall design Boart · patented
Supplier: Sino-Tech (Boart Longyear)
Threads: XQ / RQ / HD · thru-wall HT · case-hardened pin
Effect: weight ≈ V-Wall · same ID benefit · +50% tripping (Quick Descent)
Edge: gradual transition spreads stress → wear-life & stiffness > V-Wall
Cost: premium

Reading tip: OD alone misleads — also compare Body ID, Joint ID, wall thickness, thread profile, and torque / pullback / depth rating.

1Casing Comparison by Size · Benchmark

Casing lines and stabilizes the borehole and lets the drill string pass through cleanly. Sizes nest concentrically — a larger casing is set, then a smaller bit/rod runs inside — so each size steps down in diameter (B → N → H → P). All three suppliers build to the same DCDMA flush-joint W standard, so OD/ID match; the real differences are in thread type (W vs WT/HD), threads-per-inch, steel grade, and wall thickness. Sino-Tech casing is Boart Longyear, so its column doubles as the OEM reference.

2Casing Thread: W vs WT · Choosing Between Them

Casing uses only two thread families — far simpler than drill rod. W is the industry-standard DCDMA straight-ish coarse thread; WT reuses the heavy-duty HD profile (the same one on P-size rod) for a tapered, durable, reusable joint. The choice is essentially single-use vs reusable.

Reading tip: within a size, W and WT share OD/ID — pick WT when the casing will be pulled and re-run (tapered HD joint cleans & makes up faster), pick W when it's cheaper or left in the hole.
