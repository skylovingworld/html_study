①What is this product?

Precision roller chain (ANSI) — roller · plate · pin

"Cam chain" = heavy industrial chain — the steel chains that move logs, lumber, chips and bulk material through sawmills, pulp & paper, plywood/OSB mills, and that transmit power inside that machinery. It is a consumable wear part: mills replace it on a cycle, so demand repeats.

Everything in the catalogue is built on two base constructions:

  • Precision roller chain — small-pitch, standardized (ANSI B29.1 / ISO 606). Rollers spin on bushings; used for drives and fast conveyors. Strength rule of thumb: MUTS ≈ 12,500 × pitch² (lbf).
  • Welded steel chain — large-pitch links welded from sidebars + rivet + barrel. The flagship for conveying logs / chips; sold in 10-ft lengths with bolt-on attachments.
Why a tube supplier cares: the rotating parts of a chain — rollers, bushings, barrels — are cut from seamless mechanical tube and induction-hardened. That raw material + cut-to-length + hardening is exactly what PrecisePipe sells. (§④–⑤)

Per-part manufacturing routes

Link plate / side bar
steel strip / plate→blank & punch→through-harden 32–36 Rc→shot peen (fatigue)
Pin / rivet
alloy bar (1541 · 8620 · 4140 · 4340)→cold-head or turn→induction-harden zone 50–55 Rc, ≥.100″ deep→rivet into plates
Bushing & roller TUBE PARTS
seamless mechanical tube→cut to length + bevel/deburr→case- / induction-harden→press-fit into link plates
Barrel (welded chain) TUBE PART
tube — heavy-wall seamless for Super Hog→cut to length→induction-harden mid-zone 40–45 Rc · ends left soft→weld to sidebars (certified welders, preheat 93–176 °C, 7018)
Attachment
cast / forge / machine UHMW→factory-weld to chain
Live proof. A current RFQ from a chain maker asks for exactly this barrel route: 1026 CDS seamless tube, cut to length, middle 1.25″ scan-induction-hardened to 40–45 HRC, ends left soft for welding — identical to the catalogue's barrel spec. The ends stay soft because hardened (martensitic) steel cracks when welded. The technical bar is dimensional + hardness proof, not certification.
